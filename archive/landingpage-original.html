Rebuilding the Knowledge Commons

Automation is the end state. Not the starting point.

The physical infrastructure of manufacturing is being rebuilt. Automated plants are being funded, industries brought home, and workforces hired. The knowledge infrastructure isn't following the physical.

You cannot automate decisions that were never understood, or understand decisions that were never made explicit.

We start with the understanding. The knowledge.

The Problem

Confident, fast, and wrong.

Plants don't lack data. They lack the context that gives it meaning. And what context exists is scattered across SOPs, logs, manuals, and a dozen disconnected systems. The most valuable part isn't in any of them. It's in the heads of the people who run the line.

A line ran slow. The data records the deviation; only the operator knows it was a new product, a deliberate quality tradeoff, or a downstream constraint that made speed unnecessary.

This is why generic systems struggle and experienced operators outperform them. And it's why AI tools deployed on plant floors are dangerous without it. Pulling from fragmented, unverified sources, they produce outputs that look optimal on paper and fail in practice.

Documents go stale.

SOPs and manuals capture the procedure, never the judgment. They're out of date the moment practice changes.

Chatbots just retrieve.

RAG assistants return what's already written and guess at the rest. They can't capture the unwritten, and can't tell you what to trust.

Tribal knowledge walks out.

The why, the exceptions, the tells. The most valuable knowledge lives in people, and leaves when they do.

How MD1 Works

Knowledge is captured,
structured, and deployed.

Four steps that turn fragmented information and tacit expertise into a living, trusted knowledge base.

Step 01

Ingest & Structure

Point MD1 at your documents and systems. It turns them into structured, tagged knowledge cards (K-Cards).

Step 02

Elicit

It draws out the unwritten layer from your experts (the why, the when-not, the judgment) by reacting to real knowledge, not asking them to start from a blank page.

Step 03

Verify

Your people confirm, correct, and challenge. Every claim carries its source and a trust level. Knowledge evolves and is always traceable.

Step 04

Compound

It improves with every use and becomes the trusted knowledge that is understandable and used by both your people and your machines.

01 / Product The Knowledge Bank

Start with one process worth proving.

A focused engagement on one well-documented, expert-rich area. We sit with your team, ingest your documentation, capture what isn't written down, and stand up a living knowledge base in weeks.

Discover · 1–2 weeks. We sit with your operators and find the knowledge worth capturing. A process where the experts are senior, the stakes are real, and the documentation is incomplete.

Prove · 2–4 weeks. We ingest your documentation, populate the knowledge base, and work alongside your experts to capture what isn't written down.

Grow · ongoing. Broaden across processes and plants. The knowledge base compounds as your people use it.
