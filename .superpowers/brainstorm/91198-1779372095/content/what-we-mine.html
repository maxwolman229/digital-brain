What “mining” means

MD1 does not just log chats. It extracts the plant context that sits around the written knowledge in the RAG system.

Conversation signals the SDK mines

Adjacent operating context

“The SOP says X, but on Line 4 we only do that when humidity is high.”

Corrections and disagreement

“No, that’s wrong.” “That answer misses the grade change.” “We stopped doing that after the 2023 incident.”

Process exceptions

When rules change by product, equipment state, shift practice, supplier, season, or failure mode.

Sentiment and trust

Frustration, confidence, skepticism, repeated re-asking, abandonment, escalation, and positive reinforcement.

Missing-source demand

Questions the bot cannot answer because the underlying RAG corpus lacks the right source, chunk, or metadata.

Expert and domain patterns

Who gets asked, who corrects answers, which domains depend on unwritten judgment, and where knowledge risk is concentrated.

One-pager definition

  • “Mining means extracting the context around your documented knowledge: corrections, exceptions, confidence signals, expert judgment, repeated gaps, and plant-specific decision logic.”
  • “The SDK turns those signals into reviewed knowledge candidates and RAG improvement tasks.”

Important distinction

  • RAG retrieves what is already written down
  • MD1 mines what conversations reveal is missing, wrong, local, disputed, or known only by experienced people
